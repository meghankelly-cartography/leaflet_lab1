History of the Crisis

Since March of 2011, Syria has faced civil unrest leading to what the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) calls “the biggest humanitarian emergency of our era” (UNHCR). UNHCR reports that nearly 250,000 lives have been lost, roughly 6.6 million Syrians have been internally displaced, and over 4.7 million Syrians have fled the country to neighboring countries—Turkey, Jordan, Lebanon, and Iraq—and beyond to the European Union (UNHCR 2016; OCHA 2016). These numbers, however, only document registered refugees. The number of Syrians abroad is expected to be much higher.

Influx into Europe

Since March of 2011, Syria has faced civil unrest leading to what the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) calls “the biggest humanitarian emergency of our era” (UNHCR 2014). Violence has led to nearly 250,000 lives lost so far and millions have fled their homes. UNHCR reports that roughly 6.5 million Syrians have been internally displaced and over 4.5 million Syrians have fled the country to neighboring countries—Turkey, Jordan, Lebanon, and Iraq—and beyond to the European Union (UNHCR 2015). These numbers, however, only document registered refugees. The number of Syrians abroad is expected to be much higher. Women and children make up roughly three quarters of Syrian refugees (UNHCR 2014).
